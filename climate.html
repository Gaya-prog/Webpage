Climate Change & Agriculture

What is Climate Change?

  • Long-term shift in the Earth's overall weather patterns and temperatures
  • Caused by human activities [burning fossil fuels, deforestation, agricultural activities, and industrial processes]

How Climate Change takes place?

  • Greenhouse gases trap heat in the atmosphere
  • Trapped heat warms the Earth's surface to cause a rise in global temperature

Why care to mitigate climate change?

  • Protect the planet's ecosystem & biodiversity
  • Prevent extreme weather events
  • Protect human health
  • Ensure food and water security

Agriculture's Contribution to Global Greenhouse Gas Emissions (GHG)

According to the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), agriculture accounts for approximately 24%, nearly a quarter of global greenhouse gas emissions

  • cultivation of crops
  • livestock production
  • deforestation for agricultural purposes
  • management of agricultural waste

    • Climate Change and Agriculture in Southeast Asia

    Southeast Asia, home to millions of smallholder farmers, is highly vulnerable to climate change...

    • Flooding and Typhoons: Frequent tropical storms and flooding damage crops like rice, corn, and vegetables.
    • Droughts and Water Stress: In regions like Southeast Asia's dry zones...
    • Rice Production: Rising temperatures and changes in rainfall threaten rice yields...
    • Sea Level Rise: Coastal agriculture, such as rice fields in Vietnam...
    • Pests and Diseases: Warmer and wetter conditions are helping pests and diseases thrive...

    Climate Change and Agriculture in Malaysia

    Malaysia’s agriculture sector, which includes palm oil, rubber, rice, and other staple crops, is highly vulnerable...

    • Rice Production: Erratic rainfall patterns and rising temperatures affect planting cycles...
    • Oil Palm: Changes in rainfall and increased droughts threaten oil palm production...
    • Rubber: Rising temperatures and changing rainfall disrupt latex production...
    • Flooding and Storms: Coastal farming areas growing fruits and vegetables are at risk...
    • Pests and Plant Diseases: Warmer temperatures spread pests like the Rhinoceros beetle...

    Call to Action

    Sustainable Agricultural Practices

    Adopt no-till farming, agroforestry, and rotational grazing

    Low-Carbon Technologies

    Precision agriculture reduces the need for fertilizers and water

    Reducing Food Waste

    Food waste mitigation in supply chains and better consumer habits

    Shift to Plant-Based Diets

    Reduce meat consumption and increase the production of plant-based food
